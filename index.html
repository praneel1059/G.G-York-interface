G.G (Go Green) York

Human-Computer Interaction Group Project
University of York (2024–2025)

This project was designed to help students exchange and donate reusable items through a user-centered interface, developed as part of the HCIN module.

Reflection & Strengths

Working on the Human–Computer Interaction group project helped me discover how I approach challenges, learn new tools, and collaborate with others. It pushed me to grow not only as a designer but also as a teammate, and these experiences have shaped how I think about design development today.

Innovative Thinker

During the project, I aimed to design an app that was both distinctive and easy to use. I introduced features such as a scroll-based product feed and a simple traffic-light system (red, yellow, green) to show the status of product requests. These ideas made the interface feel more intuitive and helped users navigate the app with clarity and ease.

Agile Learner

As the lead designer, I had to learn Figma from scratch — it was completely new to me. I taught myself through online tutorials and a lot of trial and error, which really tested my patience but also showed me how much I enjoy learning by doing. What kept me going was the shared goal of bringing our team’s vision to life, and seeing the final prototype come together exactly as we imagined was incredibly rewarding.

Relationship Builder

Collaboration was at the heart of our project. When a teammate mentioned that a few elements were missing just before the deadline, I made sure to listen and understand their perspective rather than react defensively. We discussed it, filled in the gaps, and refined the design together. Beyond the work itself, I tried to connect with my teammates on a personal level — learning about their interests and ideas helped build trust and make collaboration feel more natural. That experience reminded me of the value of empathy and openness when working as a team, especially under pressure.

Overall, this project taught me how creativity, adaptability, and collaboration come together to create meaningful, user-centered design.

Designed using Figma · Evaluated through Collaborative Heuristic Evaluation · Based on Nielsen’s Heuristics and Norman’s Principles of Design.
